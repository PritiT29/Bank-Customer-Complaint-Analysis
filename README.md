# Bank-Customer-Complaint-Analysis
Operational Efficiency & Consumer Sentiment Dashboard

Project Overview
This project analyzes a dataset of over 86,000 bank customer complaints to identify operational bottlenecks and improve the customer experience. The analysis focuses on resolution speed, the most frequent areas of dispute, and how customers prefer to communicate with the institution.

Business Questions Addressed:
Which banking products or services generate the most friction?

How effective is the bank at resolving issues within a timely manner?

What is the most common resolution outcome for the customer?

Which communication channels (Web, Phone, Mail) are most active?

Tech Stack
Visualization: Tableau (Interactive Dashboard) [https://public.tableau.com/views/BankCustomersComplaints_16812365712350/Dashboard1?:language=en-US&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link]

Data Processing: SQL (BigQuery / PostgreSQL)

Data Source: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) Complaint Database

Executive Summary of Findings
1. Performance Metrics (KPIs)
High Efficiency: The bank maintains a 98.90% Timely Response rate.

Low Backlog: Only 0.38% of complaints are currently "In Progress," suggesting a highly responsive customer service infrastructure.

Volume Trends: Total complaints reached 86,893, with a rolling 12-month average of 20,202.

2. Top Issues & Friction Points
Billing Disputes are the #1 cause of complaints (14,688 cases).

Identity Theft & Fraud rank in the top three, indicating a need for improved security protocols and customer education.

3. Resolution Analysis
75.59% of cases are resolved with a formal explanation, successfully closing the loop with the customer.

Only 3.64% of cases required monetary relief, while 13.43% were settled with non-monetary relief (e.g., fee waivers or credit corrections).

Strategic Recommendations
1. Reduce Billing Friction: Since "Billing Disputes" are the primary issue, the bank should simplify digital statements and provide a "One-Click Dispute" feature in the mobile app.

2. Optimize Digital Channels: With 68.92% of complaints coming via the Web, the bank should invest in AI-driven chatbots to resolve simple billing queries instantly.

3. Fraud Prevention: Given the high volume of Identity Theft complaints, a proactive SMS alert system for unusual activity could prevent complaints before they happen.
